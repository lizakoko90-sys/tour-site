Three Days in the North:
Murmansk, Teriberka and Waterfalls

"Weekend in Murmansk" is a ready-made 3-day itinerary that brings our most popular excursions together into one action-packed journey. The perfect choice for anyone who wants to get to know the Arctic and see its main sights in a short time.

Day one — a city sightseeing tour of Murmansk. Five Corners Square, the Alyosha monument (closed for restoration until 2028), the nuclear-powered icebreaker Lenin, the Waiting Woman monument and panoramic views over the Kola Bay.

Day two — a trip to Teriberka. A voyage out into the Barents Sea in search of whales, a sandy beach, the swing, the throne, the ship graveyard, the "Dragon Eggs" and the Batareysky waterfall.

Day three — the scenic waterfalls of Western Murman on the road to Norway. Sites of military glory from the Second World War and the stark beauty of northern nature.

A certified local guide accompanies you throughout the entire route. All transfers are in comfortable crossovers and minivans.

Tour programme

DAY 1 · CITY SIGHTSEEING TOUR OF MURMANSK
CITY
Start
We start from your hotel
We pick you up at your hotel or apartment — we set off at any time that suits you. The city sightseeing tour takes 3–4 hours.
The city centre: Five Corners Square (Murmansk's central plaza)
We begin exploring the city where it all started. We stroll through the central square, see the Kirov Palace of Culture and the Cod Monument.
Memorial to sailors who died in peacetime
A lighthouse memorial, a ship's anchor, a fragment of the conning tower of the nuclear submarine Kursk and the Church of the Saviour on the Waters.
The Monument to the Defenders of the Soviet Arctic
Locals affectionately call it "Alyosha" — a huge 42-metre statue and the city's true landmark. (Currently closed for restoration until 2028 — we take you to a spot with a great view of it.)
The Sea Terminal and the nuclear icebreaker Lenin
The world's first nuclear-powered icebreaker — a unique museum with a rich history of conquering the Arctic.
The Waiting Woman monument
A wonderful viewpoint over the port and the waters of the Kola Bay.
The bridge across the Kola Bay
We drive across one of the longest road bridges above the Arctic Circle — with impressive views over the bay.
The Abram-Mys memorial complex
The most beautiful viewpoint over Murmansk and the Kola Bay. The evening is free.
DAY 2 · TERIBERKA AND WHALE WATCHING
SEA
07:30
Departure from Murmansk to Teriberka
About 120 km of scenic tundra with stops at the most beautiful spots:
  • the Kola Wind Farm;
  • the seidas on Mount Eriveyv.
The drive takes 2–2.5 hours, arriving in Teriberka around 09:30.
10:00
A safety briefing, meeting the captain and heading out into the Barents Sea (2 hours) in search of whales. Most often we spot minke and humpback whales, kept company by dolphins and seals. We provide life jackets for everyone, and the guide carries seasickness remedies. There is a good chance of meeting whales, but nature is beyond our control — a 100% guarantee is impossible. Even so, the sea voyage itself is something you will remember for the rest of your life.
12:10
A food break at a panoramic restaurant on the ocean shore — fresh fish dishes and local seafood.
13:30
A walk along the sandy coast: the giant swing by the ocean, the wooden throne, an old fishing boat. A husky farm is available on request.
14:30
The graveyard of Pomor ships from the 1950s — trawlers frozen in time, an atmospheric photo spot.
15:00
The Teriberka Nature Park: the pebble beach known as the "Dragon Eggs" and the roaring Batareysky waterfall that plunges straight into the ocean (~7 km along eco-trails).
17:00
We set off back to Murmansk, returning to your hotel around 19:00.
DAY 3 · WATERFALLS OF THE KOLA REGION
WATER
09:00
Meeting your guide at your hotel
We head towards the Norwegian border, to the waterfalls of Western Murman.
09:40
The waterfall on the Ura river
A scenic waterfall and a walk through the coniferous forest — the freshness and silence of northern nature.
10:30
The Valley of Glory and WWII positions
A memorial to the heroes of the Second World War, preserved fortifications, a cave hospital and a soldiers' dugout.
12:30
The Zapadnaya Litsa river
If we are lucky, we will see wild salmon leaping over the rapids of the rushing river — a true northern wonder.
13:30
Lunch
We relax in a cosy café serving northern cuisine — we try the famous halibut buns.
14:30
The waterfall on the Titovka river
One of the most spectacular waterfalls of Western Murman — stunning final shots.
17:30
Return to Murmansk. We will help with a transfer to the airport or railway station if it is time to leave.
