A Real Northern Lights
Hunt near Murmansk

Join us for a real northern lights hunt — for emotions that will stay with you forever. We drive out of town, away from the lights, in search of this unique natural phenomenon: we wait for clear skies, photograph you on a professional camera, warm up with hot tea and northern liqueurs — and admire the true wonder of the polar sky together.

We pick you up right from your hotel or apartment and take you to the best dark spots on the Kola Peninsula, where the chances of clear skies are highest. We keep an eye on the solar-activity and cloud-cover forecast to choose the most promising night.

An honest word about the aurora 🌙

The northern lights are a living natural phenomenon, and no one in the world can promise them 100%: it all comes down to solar activity and the weather, and nature is beyond our control. But we do everything in our power to make it happen for you. And if the aurora does not show up this time — a second hunt costs just 3 000 ₽ instead of 4 000 ₽, to give the wonder one more chance.

Good to know

The aurora-hunting season on the Kola Peninsula runs from late August to April, when the nights turn dark. The lights are most often caught between September and March. We head out every evening the weather allows.

The northern lights are a living natural phenomenon, and no one in the world can promise them 100%: it all comes down to solar activity and the weather, and nature is beyond our control. But we do everything in our power: we watch the forecast, pick the clearest night and take you to the best dark spots far from the city lights. And if the aurora does not show up this time — a repeat hunt costs just 3 000 ₽ instead of 4 000 ₽.

We confirm the exact departure time on the day of the hunt — it depends on the forecast. We usually set out between 8:00 and 9:30 pm and return around 1:00 am. Sometimes the hunt runs until 2:00 or 3:00 am if solar activity is weak or the sky is overcast — we wait and catch a clear window.

Dress very warmly and in layers: a warm windproof jacket, thermal underwear, warm trousers, a hat, gloves and waterproof winter boots. It is colder out in nature at night than in the city, and you will be standing still. Bring a thermos with a hot drink — even though we serve tea and northern liqueurs. If you would like to combine this with a daytime programme, take a look at the "All of Teriberka in One Day" tour.

The first aurora hunt costs 4 000 ₽ per person, a repeat hunt 3 000 ₽ per person. Payment is made before the trip — in cash or by bank transfer through your banking app.

Send a request through the form on the site, message us on Telegram or give us a call. We will get in touch, suggest the best nights based on the forecast and add you to a group.
