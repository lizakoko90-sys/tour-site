A tour to the waterfalls of the Murmansk Region
— towards Norway

We invite you on a thrilling journey through the western part of the Murmansk Region — to the place where waterfalls roar and the forests are full of berries and mushrooms, like a true northern garden. This route follows the road leading to the Norwegian border and combines stunning natural scenery, historical depth and rare encounters with wildlife.

🌲 What awaits you:

🔹 We will see two picturesque waterfalls — on the Ura and Titovka rivers. Their turbulent flow and pristine power will not leave you indifferent.
🔹 We will walk along the bank of the Zapadnaya Litsa river, where with a bit of luck you can watch wild salmon leap into the air as they clear the rapids.
🔹 We will visit sites of military glory — exploring the surviving German defensive fortifications from the Great Patriotic War (WWII).
🔹 We will enjoy the silence and freshness of the northern forest, rich in berries and mushrooms — a perfect place for lovers of nature and solitude.

This trip is ideal for anyone who wants to discover real northern nature, immerse themselves in the atmosphere of remote corners and feel the power of the Kola Peninsula.

This is a comfortable tour by car along the road towards Norway, where drives between locations alternate with walks to the waterfalls and sites of military glory.

Part of the route runs along a forest trail and slopes, so comfortable footwear and a moderate level of fitness matter. No special equipment is required, but the walks to the waterfalls will call for a little stamina.

The route is a great fit for anyone who wants to see real northern nature far from the city and feel the pristine power of the Kola Peninsula.

Good to know

A bottle of water, sunglasses and sunscreen, and a charged phone or camera — the views will be stunning! In summer be sure to bring insect repellent or spray (mosquitoes and midges are possible along the route), as well as a warm layer of clothing in case the weather turns cool.

Be sure to wear comfortable shoes — part of the route runs along a forest trail and slopes. Dress for the weather: it is a good idea to bring a windbreaker or raincoat in case the weather changes. The weather in the North is changeable, and even in summer it can be cool (from +10 to +20 °C, especially near the sea and in the mountains) — bring a warm layer just in case.
